The profile page is where you will be able to access your and your friends' personal profile and stats. You can also access your personal goals through the profile.

Edit

The picture, name, bio, and diet are all able to be edited by clicking on the edit button in the upperleft corner.

Stats

This brings you to another screen, where you can see trends in your diet on a daily, weekly, or monthly basis and also see an overall trend or by food group.

Personal Goals

This brings you to another screen, where you can see a list of your personal goals and either add or delete goals. The goals are categorized into "all" goals, "current" goasl, or "complete" goals.

Challenge Stats

Gives you a a brief overview of your challenge statistics.

Send Challenge

Disabled in your own profile, in other users' profiles, you can send them a challenge as this will redirect you to the send challenge form.

What is a challenge?

Participants must reach the daily recommended intake of all food groups to keep the streak going. You can include any number of friends in your challenge.

Checking challenge stats

You can check your current challenges under the current challenges page. You can check out a line graph showing your or your friends progress.

Fruits

Daily Intake

Recommended Foods

General Info

According to the USDA, fruits are low in fat, sodium, and calories. In addition, they are sources of potassium, dietary fiber, vitamin C, and folate, which are crucial since:

  • Potassium helps maintain healthy blood pressure
  • Dietary fiber helps reduce blood cholesterol
  • Vitamin C is needed for growth and repair of bodily tissues
Overall, including fruits in diets may reduce risk for heart disease, protect against some types of cancer, and reduce the risk of developing kidney stones and bone loss.

Vegetables

Daily Intake

Recommended Foods

General Info

According to the USDA, vegetables are low in fat and calories. They are important sources of potassium, dietary fiber, folate, vitamin A, and vitamin C, which are crucial since:

  • Potassium helps maintain healthy blood pressure
  • Dietary fiber helps reduce blood cholesterol
  • Folate helps red blood cells form
  • Vitamin A keeps eyes and skin healthy
  • Vitamin C helps heal cuts and wounds
Overall, including vegetables in diets may reduce the risk for heart disease, protect against types of cancer, and lower calorie intake.

Grains

Daily Intake

Recommended Foods

General Info

According to the USDA, grains are important sources of dietary fiber, B vitamins (thiamin, riboflavin, niacin, and folate) and minerals (iron, magnesium, and selenium), which are crucial since:

  • Dietary fiber may help reduce blood cholesterol levels
  • B vitamins needed in metabolism
  • Iron needed to carry oxygen to blood
Overall, including grains in diets may reduce risk for heart disease, reduce constipation, help with weight management, and may reduce constipation.

Protein

Daily Intake

Recommended Foods

General Info

Sources of protein include meat, poultry, seafood, peas, and nuts. According to the USDA, protein is important for building bones, muscles, cartilage, skin, and blood. In addition to B vitamins and minerals, protein also contains vitamin E and omega-3 fatty acids, which are crucial since:

  • B vitamins help the body release energy
  • Omega-3 fatty acids reduce the risk of heart disease

Dairy

Daily Intake

Recommended Foods

General Info

According to the USDA, dairy is an important source of calcium, which is used for building bones and teeth and in maintaning bone mass. Some products like yogurt and soymilk provide potassium. Overall, consumption of dairy products is most crucial for children since calcium plays an important role in building bone mass.

For those that cannot consume milk products, alternatives include:

  • Calcium-fortified juices, cereal, breads, rice milk, or almond milk
  • Soy products
  • Kale and bok choy
